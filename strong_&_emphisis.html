WILLIAMS SHAKESPEARE

William Shakespeare, Sonnet 33. Even so my sun one early morn did shine, With all triumphant splendour on my brow; But out, alack, he was but one hour mine, The region cloud hath mask’d him from me now Golden slumbers kiss your eyes, Smiles awake you when you rise; Sleep, pretty wantons, do not cry, And I will sing a lullaby, Rock them, rock them, lullaby …
‘Golden Slumbers’ is a beautiful lullaby from Thoth Henry Chettle and William Haughton. This is one of the most soothing and beautiful poems of the Renaissance – an adamsen
